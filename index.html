Overview of SMSF Property Investments

Since the introduction of Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangements (LRBAs) in 2007, SMSFs have enabled Australians to leverage superannuation for property purchases. This allows for tax-efficient growth through rental yields and capital appreciation, all while adhering to strict superannuation regulations.

Key advantages include concessional tax rates on income (15%) and reduced capital gains tax (one-third discount after 12 months, effective 10% rate in pension phase).

Buying and Selling Property via SMSF

Buying: Ensure the property aligns with your SMSF's investment strategy. Use an LRBA for borrowing, involving a bare trust to hold the asset during the loan term.

Selling: Handle capital gains within the fund, potentially triggering tax events. Compliance with arm's length rules is crucial.

  • Pre-purchase: Due diligence on title, zoning, and SMSF compliance.
  • During transaction: Manage stamp duty, GST (if applicable), and contract execution by SMSF trustee.
  • Post-transaction: Update investment registers and financial statements.

Compliance Essentials for SMSF Property

SMSFs must comply with SIS Act requirements:

  • Sole purpose test: Investments solely for retirement benefits.
  • Arm's length transactions: All dealings at market value.
  • Prohibited acquisitions: No residential property from related parties.
  • LRBA specifics: Single acquirable asset, limited recourse loan.
  • Audit and reporting: Annual audits by approved auditors.

Non-compliance can lead to penalties up to 45% tax on benefits or fund disqualification.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Can my SMSF buy a property I already own? No, acquisitions from related parties are prohibited for residential properties.
  • What are the borrowing limits? Loans are subject to lender policies and SMSF liquidity requirements.
  • How is stamp duty calculated? Based on the property's value, with potential concessions for SMSFs in some states.
